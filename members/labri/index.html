Formal Methods @ LaBRI

We develop formal computer science methods and tools related to the semantics, verification, control, and synthesis of Boolean networks and related formalisms. Our research interests include the following topics:

  • Most Permissive Boolean networks (Paulevé et al, 2020) which bring formal guarantees for the abstract modeling of quantitative systems. Moreover, they offer a much lower complexity than traditional (a)synchronous Boolean networks for verifying trajectories and attractors. They can address Boolean networks at the genome-scale, with several thousands of nodes.

  • Reprogramming of Boolean networks to predict control strategies for driving a Boolean network from one attractor to another. With collaborators in theoretical and experimental biology, we investigate applications in cellular reprogramming. See notebooks for Boolean network reprogramming.

  • Synthesis (inference) of Boolean networks from architecture (influence graph) and dynamical properties using Answer-Set Programming. We are currently investigating applications to single-cell data of cellular differentiation processes.

We are involved in the CoLoMoTo Docker environment and Interactive Notebook. We develop the tools Pint devoted to the analysis of reachability in asynchronous automata networks; mpbn for the analysis of reachability and attractors in Most Permissive Boolean networks; and BoNesis for the synthesis of Most Permissive Boolean networks from architecture and dynamical properties.
